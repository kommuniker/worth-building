# Unit Economics (FICTIONAL EXAMPLE)

> Illustrative only. All figures are invented.

| Metric | Value | Assumption |
|--------|-------|------------|
| Monthly price | $200 per therapist | Midpoint of $150-300 hypothesis. |
| CAC | $600 per clinic | Founder-led outreach plus narrow paid tests. |
| Monthly churn | 3% | Vertical SaaS assumption after year one. |
| Average lifetime | 33 months | 1 divided by monthly churn. |
| LTV | About $20,000 per clinic | $7,560 ARPA x 33/12 x 95% retention-adjusted gross margin proxy. |
| LTV:CAC | About 33:1 | Strong if CAC stays founder-led/inbound. |
| Payback period | About 0.9 months | First month revenue nearly covers CAC. |
| Gross margin | About 70% | AI inference plus compliance infrastructure cost. |
| Capital needed to first proof | About $3,000 | Landing page, ads, and BAA-covered pilot setup. |
| Months to first meaningful proof | 2-3 months | RAT in 2 weeks; design partners inside 90 days. |

## Interpretation

The economics work if the premium vertical price holds and acquisition
does not become sales-heavy. The main economic failure mode is pricing
collapse to horizontal scribe levels combined with high compliance cost.
