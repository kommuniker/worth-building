# Customer Segments (FICTIONAL EXAMPLE)

> Illustrative only.

## Primary Segment

Independent US outpatient PT clinics with 2-5 therapists. The owner is
often also a practicing therapist, so the user pain and economic buyer
are close together. This segment can decide quickly and does not require
enterprise procurement.

## Secondary Segments

- PT clinic chains with 6-25 therapists. Larger budgets, but longer
  buying cycles and more operational stakeholders.
- Chiropractic and sports-rehab clinics. Adjacent note burden, but the
  clinical workflow and terminology diverge enough that they should wait.
- Hospital outpatient PT departments. High volume, but enterprise sales,
  compliance review, and EHR integration are too heavy for the beachhead.

## Buying Dynamics

Small clinic owners buy when the pitch connects documentation time to
retention, claim quality, and therapist capacity. They are unlikely to
run a formal procurement process, but they will demand trust signals:
HIPAA handling, clinician review, and proof that the notes fit PT
workflows.
