# Market Overview (FICTIONAL EXAMPLE)

> Illustrative only. All figures are invented for the example and are not
> meant to reflect real market research.

## Market Definition

US outpatient physical therapy clinics, starting with independent clinics
with 2-5 therapists. The buyer is the clinic owner or clinical director.
The product competes with manual documentation time, EHR dictation, and
horizontal clinical AI scribes.

## TAM

Approximately 40,000 outpatient PT clinics in the US x average 5
therapists = about 200,000 therapists. At $1,800-3,600 ARPA per
therapist, the software TAM is about $360M-$720M. This is a fictional
top-down estimate used to demonstrate format.

Services-TAM caveat: because the product replaces therapist
documentation time, the addressable value is larger than software spend
alone. A 15-therapist clinic spending 30-60 minutes per therapist per
day on notes is effectively spending a meaningful labor budget on the
problem.

## SAM

Independent US clinics with 2-5 therapists: about 22,000 clinics x 3.5
average therapists = about 77,000 therapists. At $1,800-3,600 ARPA per
therapist, SAM is about $140M-$280M.

This is the owner-buyer segment where fast decisions are plausible
without enterprise procurement.

## SOM

A realistic 3-year beachhead target is 3% of SAM: about 660 clinics x
3.5 therapists x $2,400 midpoint ARPA = about $5.5M ARR. This is
bootstrap-compatible and does not require venture-scale adoption to be
interesting.

## Market Timing

- Ambient clinical AI scribing became familiar to buyers in 2024-2025.
- Staffing pressure makes administrative time savings easier to justify.
- Therapy coding changes create a window where documentation workflows
  are already being reconsidered.
- Horizontal scribe players are prioritizing larger physician markets,
  creating a short window for vertical specialization.

## Regulatory Landscape

HIPAA applies. The product needs BAA-covered infrastructure, clinician
review before note submission, audit logging, and clear positioning as a
drafting assistant rather than an autonomous clinical record author. FDA
classification is not expected in this fictional example because the
product is documentation support, not diagnosis or therapy.

## Geographic Considerations

Focus on the US for v1. EU and Canada add privacy-law complexity and
should wait until the US beachhead is validated.
