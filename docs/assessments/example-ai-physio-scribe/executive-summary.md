# Executive Summary (FICTIONAL EXAMPLE)

## Verdict

**Validate first**

## Decision Rule

Qualification score **62/100** (Validate-first band is 55-69). Defensibility
score **54/100** (moderate). No automatic disqualifier. One `Concern` gate:
founder-market fit. The cheapest next proof is a 2-week landing-page
test to validate that PT clinic owners respond to the problem framing
with the same urgency GP doctors did.

## Why

The mechanical case is strong: horizontal AI scribes have proven the
technology works and the buying behavior in clinical settings exists.
Vertical depth (PT-specific transcript corpus, CPT coding, exercise
prescriptions) is a defensible moat that horizontal incumbents are
unlikely to prioritize. The team can build the vertical product faster
than horizontal players can add PT-specific modules.

What's not yet proven: that independent PT clinic owners — as distinct
from GP doctors — experience documentation pain with sufficient urgency
to pay $150-300/therapist/month for a specialist tool. GP doctor
urgency is documented; PT urgency is inferred by analogy. This is the
single assumption that could kill the idea.

## What Must Be True

- PT clinic owners recognize the pain as significant enough to pay a
  separate line item on top of EHR spend.
- At least 2-3 independent PT clinic owners will agree to become design
  partners within 90 days of first outreach.
- Horizontal incumbents do not add meaningful PT support in the next 12
  months (reasonably likely — PT is a small vertical for them).

## Immediate Next Step

Launch a landing page test targeting independent PT clinic owners, with
a $500 LinkedIn ad budget over 2 weeks. Pass criterion: ≥ 5% CTA-click
rate from qualified traffic. Pass triggers proceeding to concierge
pilots. Fail triggers killing or pivoting the idea. See
`rat-experiment.md` for the full experiment design.
