# Red & Green Flags (FICTIONAL EXAMPLE)

> Illustrative only.

## Green Flags

- **Clear wedge.** Independent 2-5 therapist PT clinics is a real, narrow,
  under-served segment — owner-buyer means fast decisions, not enterprise
  procurement.
- **Vertical depth is a real moat.** PT-specific transcripts, exercise
  library, CPT coding are all genuinely different from physician
  workflows — not just a reskin.
- **Agent model, not tool.** The product replaces a task (documentation +
  coding + exercise prescription), not a step. Higher embedding, higher
  switching cost, higher willingness to pay.
- **Proven technology.** Ambient scribing is revenue-positive in the
  physician market; what's being tested is fit, not feasibility.
- **Cheap validation path.** The riskiest assumption can be tested for
  under $1K in 2 weeks.
- **Timing is favorable.** Staffing crisis makes efficiency visible to
  owners; CPT changes create retraining window.
- **Team is technically well-matched.** HCI + AI/ML + bootstrap
  constraints align with the product shape (self-serve, PLG,
  small-clinic PLG).

## Red Flags

- **Founder-market fit is the weakest dimension.** No prior PT clinical
  network, no default credibility with clinic owners. Every initial
  conversation is cold.
- **Urgency is inferred by analogy, not measured.** GP documentation pain
  is proven; PT documentation pain may be lower-magnitude because
  sessions are shorter and notes are briefer. This is the RAT.
- **Horizontal incumbents are a latent threat.** ScribeMD Pro or NotePal
  deciding to add PT is a real risk. Mitigation: vertical depth + data
  flywheel can outrun generic additions, but the head start window is
  finite.
- **HIPAA compliance is non-trivial for 2 people.** Not a blocker but a
  real cost — BAA-compliant infrastructure, malpractice review of notes.
  Must be treated as a first-class cost, not a line item.
- **Outcome dependency on AI accuracy.** A single bad note that causes a
  denied claim creates churn. AI reliability risk is elevated in a
  regulated clinical setting.
- **Data flywheel requires real usage.** Moat doesn't exist until
  clinics are actually using the product and correcting outputs. In the
  first 6 months, the moat is potential, not real.
