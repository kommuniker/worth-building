# One-Pager — AI Scribe For PT Clinics (FICTIONAL EXAMPLE)

> Illustrative only.

## Idea

An ambient AI scribe for physical therapists: listens to the session and
writes the SOAP note, CPT codes, and exercise prescription — no typing.

## Customer

Independent outpatient PT clinics, 2-5 therapists, US-based. Owner-buyer,
same-day decision possible.

## Pain

30-60 minutes/day of documentation per therapist. #1 reason therapists
cite for burnout and late departures. Horizontal scribes (built for
doctors) don't speak PT.

## Wedge

PT-specific vertical AI: fine-tuned against PT transcripts + CPT codes +
exercise libraries. Horizontal scribes cannot compete on vertical depth
without rebuilding their product.

## Right To Win

Technical: 2-person team can ship vertical AI faster than horizontal
incumbents can add PT. Distribution: PLG + state association newsletters
fits the team. Gap: no direct PT domain network — requires design
partners within 90 days.

## Defensibility

- Data flywheel: proprietary PT transcript corpus + correction signal
- Vertical workflow embedding (CPT codes, exercise prescription)
- Trust / HIPAA compliance as entry barrier
- Agent (not tool) model — higher switching cost

## Market

~40K US outpatient PT clinics (fictional estimate). SAM for 2-5 therapist
clinics: ~22K. Realistic SOM at 3% over 3 years: ~600 clinics, ~2,000
therapists, ~$3-6M ARR range.

## Competition

Horizontal scribes (ScribeMD Pro, NotePal) don't support PT well;
vertical PT tools (FlowState-PT, Clinic-AI Note) focus on chiropractic or
are scheduling-first, not scribing. Clear opening.

## Capital And Validation

Bootstrap-only. Validation: landing page + 3-4 concierge pilots. Budget
under $1K. Proof expected in 90 days.

## Decision Logic

- Qualification score: **62/100**
- Defensibility score: **54/100**
- Critical gate: Founder-market fit (`Concern` — no prior PT network)
- Core unvalidated assumption: PT clinic owners have urgent pain at a
  different magnitude than GP doctors. Fixable with a 2-week landing-page
  test.

## Decision

**Validate first.**
